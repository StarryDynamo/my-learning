## 💡 Concept

The Platform Envelopment Trap occurs when established platforms aggressively expand into adjacent markets by bundling new capabilities, creating a strategic paradox: the very success of envelopment often undermines the platform's core value proposition. Unlike simple feature creep, envelopment involves systematically absorbing entire categories that previously thrived in the platform's ecosystem. The trap emerges because each successful envelopment reduces third-party innovation incentives, gradually hollowing out the ecosystem that made the platform valuable initially.

This creates a delayed feedback loop where short-term competitive gains mask long-term strategic vulnerabilities. The platform becomes increasingly dependent on its own innovation capacity while losing the distributed creativity that originally fueled its growth. Companies fall into this trap because envelopment delivers immediate market share gains and appears to strengthen competitive moats, but it ultimately transforms dynamic ecosystems into static, centrally-controlled product suites that struggle to adapt to unexpected market shifts.

## 🔍 Real-World Example

Cursor's rapid expansion from AI coding assistant to comprehensive development orchestration platform exemplifies this dynamic in real-time. In early 2026, Cursor added over 30 enterprise integrations with major platforms like Atlassian, GitLab, and Datadog, positioning itself as the central hub for AI-augmented development workflows. While this expansion delivers immediate value to enterprise customers and creates switching costs, it also signals to potential ecosystem partners that Cursor may eventually absorb their functionality.

This mirrors Salesforce's evolution from CRM platform to business application suite. Initially, Salesforce's AppExchange thrived with thousands of third-party solutions. However, as Salesforce acquired or built competing features—marketing automation, analytics, e-commerce—many specialized vendors either pivoted away or became reluctant to invest heavily in Salesforce-specific innovations. Today, while Salesforce dominates enterprise software, its pace of breakthrough innovation has slowed compared to its platform-centric era.

## 🌉 Cross-Domain Bridge

In AI Management, this manifests as the "feature absorption dilemma" in large language model ecosystems. As foundation model providers like OpenAI expand their capabilities—adding vision, code execution, web browsing, and specialized reasoning—they systematically absorb use cases that specialized AI startups were addressing. This creates the same paradox: while users benefit from integrated capabilities, the innovation ecosystem becomes less diverse and more dependent on a few large players' roadmaps. The most innovative AI applications increasingly come from companies with access to proprietary data or unique interfaces rather than novel AI architectures, because the foundational capabilities have been enveloped by platform providers who can offer them at marginal cost.
